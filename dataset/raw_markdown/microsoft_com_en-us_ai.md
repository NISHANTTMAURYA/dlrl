### What is generative AI? How is it different from other types of AI?

[Generative AI](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/ai/ai-101/what-is-generative-ai) uses deep learning to create new content in response to natural language prompts. It can also generate text, images, music, and code.  
  
There are several types of AI, each designed for different purposes: 
    * **Traditional AI -** Automates specific tasks using predefined rules and algorithms, often used in manufacturing and data processing.
    * **Predictive AI -** Analyzes historical data to forecast future outcomes, commonly used in finance, healthcare, and marketing.
    * **Conversational AI -** Powers chatbots and virtual assistants to facilitate natural language interactions, generating human-like responses.  

Generative AI is different because it creates content rather than simply analyzing or predicting based on existing data. 

[ See AI solutions ](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/ai#ai-solutions)

### What is a Copilot?

Copilots are chat-based generative AI interactions from Microsoft. They have specialized functionality based on their users and use cases. Some Copilots are experienced standalone, while others are integrated into Microsoft products, services, and devices.  
  
Included in this portfolio is [Microsoft Copilot](https://go.microsoft.com/fwlink/?linkid=2306609), [Microsoft 365 Copilot](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/microsoft-365/copilot/copilot-for-work), [Microsoft Security Copilot](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/security/business/ai-machine-learning/microsoft-security-copilot), and [GitHub Copilot](https://azure.microsoft.com/en-us/products/github/copilot), among other generative AI solutions and experiences.

### What are the potential risks and challenges associated with implementing generative AI?

Implementing generative AI comes with several considerations, including data security, biases, misinformation, and regulatory compliance. Ensuring the security of sensitive data and managing potential biases in AI-generated content are critical concerns—in addition to challenges related to transparency, accountability, and the potential misuse of AI technologies. Microsoft’s strategy paper, [Minimize Risk and Reap the Benefits of AI](https://go.microsoft.com/fwlink/?linkid=2299956), provides a comprehensive guide to navigating the challenges and opportunities of using generative AI.

### How do I ensure the accuracy and reliability of AI-generated content? 

Ensuring the accuracy and reliability of AI-generated content requires evaluating and monitoring the performance of generative AI models. Microsoft's [Azure AI Foundry](https://azure.microsoft.com/en-us/products/ai-foundry) offers evaluation metrics that assess the quality, safety, and relevance of generated content. Techniques such as Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) can also improve accuracy by using sourced data instead of relying solely on the model's training data.
